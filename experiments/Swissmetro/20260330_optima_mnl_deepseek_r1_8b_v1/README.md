# Optima Multinomial Logit Experiment Report for deepseek-r1:8b

This archive directory records the multinomial logit (MNL) estimation exercise that uses the retained Optima human data together with the AI questionnaire data generated by `deepseek-r1:8b`. In the current repository organization, the maintained code lives in the root `scripts` directory and the maintained data package lives in `data/Swissmetro/demographic_choice_psychometric`. The present folder should therefore be interpreted as an archival trial rather than as the active development location.

The MNL exercise was estimated on two data sources. The human side uses the retained Optima benchmark data. The AI side uses the already collected deepseek questionnaire results stored in `data/Swissmetro/demographic_choice_psychometric/ai_collection_deepseek_r1_8b`. Because not every deepseek response was valid, the AI estimation used only the `568` observations with valid values for all six retained indicators and the final mode choice. Two MNL specifications were estimated on both datasets. The first specification is the basic four-parameter model with two alternative-specific constants and two generic coefficients for cost and time. The second specification augments the baseline with respondent characteristics while excluding destination and origin variables.

## Archive structure

- `outputs/human_basic` stores the human benchmark estimation for the four-parameter MNL.
- `outputs/human_user_no_od` stores the human benchmark estimation for the respondent-characteristic MNL without destination and origin variables.
- `outputs/ai_basic` stores the deepseek AI-data estimation for the four-parameter MNL.
- `outputs/ai_user_no_od` stores the deepseek AI-data estimation for the respondent-characteristic MNL without destination and origin variables.

## Main numerical results

The human benchmark behaves in the expected way. The final log likelihood improves from `-500.457` in the basic specification to `-391.212` in the respondent-characteristic specification. This improvement indicates that the retained non-destination, non-origin respondent characteristics contain meaningful explanatory power in the human sample.

The deepseek AI data behave differently, but they do not collapse as strongly as the earlier `qwen3.5:9b` MNL archive. In the AI basic specification, the final log likelihood is `-385.932`. Once respondent characteristics are introduced, the final log likelihood improves to `-336.042`. Therefore, the deepseek AI data still exhibit substantial structure, but they do not generate the near-perfect deterministic fit that previously appeared in the retained `qwen3.5:9b` archive.

## Behavioral interpretation

The basic four-parameter model highlights the central human-versus-AI difference. In the human benchmark, both the generic cost coefficient and the generic time coefficient are negative, at approximately `-0.613` and `-0.339`, respectively. This is consistent with a conventional behavioral interpretation in which greater generalized cost lowers utility.

The deepseek AI basic model also produces a negative cost coefficient, but the magnitude is much smaller at approximately `-0.197`. More importantly, the generic time coefficient becomes positive at approximately `0.189`. Thus, even though the aggregate deepseek mode shares are not far from the human benchmark, the AI trade-off structure remains behaviorally implausible. The model is effectively telling us that the deepseek responses do not encode the same disutility of travel time as the human benchmark.

The aggregate choice shares clarify this point. In the human sample, the shares are approximately `31.36%` for `PT`, `62.57%` for `CAR`, and `6.07%` for `SLOW_MODES`. In the valid deepseek AI sample, the corresponding shares are approximately `34.51%` for `PT`, `63.03%` for `CAR`, and `2.46%` for `SLOW_MODES`. Therefore, the aggregate shares are relatively close for `PT` and `CAR`, while `SLOW_MODES` remains underrepresented in the AI data. The coefficient results show that this superficial similarity in shares does not imply a successful recovery of the human utility trade-offs.

## Interpretation of the respondent-characteristic specification

The respondent-characteristic model reinforces the same conclusion. On the human side, the expanded specification improves fit in a plausible way and yields a richer but still interpretable coefficient pattern. On the AI side, the expanded specification also improves fit, but several coefficients become very large or change sign in ways that are difficult to reconcile with a stable behavioral mechanism. For example, some AI-side car-specific respondent-characteristic coefficients become extremely large, and the generic time coefficient remains behaviorally problematic. These patterns suggest that the model is fitting idiosyncratic regularities in the AI responses rather than recovering a human-like preference structure.

## Overall assessment

This archived deepseek MNL trial supports a more nuanced conclusion than the retained `qwen3.5:9b` MNL archive. Deepseek does not force the sample into an almost perfectly deterministic car-dominant pattern. Its aggregate `PT` and `CAR` shares are in fact relatively close to the human benchmark. Nevertheless, the recovered utility structure remains distinctly non-human, because the generic time coefficient is positive and the respondent-characteristic specification still drifts toward unstable coefficient patterns. Accordingly, the present archive suggests that aggregate shares alone are not an adequate criterion for judging whether an AI questionnaire has reproduced human transport preferences.
